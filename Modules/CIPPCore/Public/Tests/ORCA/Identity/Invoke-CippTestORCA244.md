Anti-spam policies should honor DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance) policies published by sending domains. When enabled, this setting respects the sender domain's DMARC policy for handling authentication failures, improving email security by enforcing the sender's preferred treatment of spoofed or forged messages.
