# Tenant MFA Report

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) adds an extra layer of security by requiring users to verify their identity through a second method — such as a phone, authenticator app, or security key — in addition to their password.

This report provides a complete overview of your tenant's MFA posture across all accounts. It shows who has registered for MFA, which enforcement method is in place (Conditional Access, Security Defaults, or Per-User MFA), and highlights any accounts that are not protected.

Accounts without MFA enforcement are significantly more vulnerable to phishing and credential-stuffing attacks.
