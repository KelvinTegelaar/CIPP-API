# Tenant Has Enabled DLP Policies Configured

Data Loss Prevention (DLP) policies detect and prevent the sharing of sensitive information — such as credit card numbers, social security numbers, or health records — across Exchange, SharePoint, OneDrive, and Teams. With Microsoft 365 Copilot in place, DLP becomes even more important: Copilot can retrieve and summarize content from across the tenant, and without DLP policies, sensitive data may be surfaced or included in Copilot-generated responses without restriction.

Having at least one enabled DLP policy signals that the tenant has begun governing its sensitive data. This test requires a Microsoft Purview or Azure Information Protection license (included in M365 Business Premium, E3, and E5) and will be skipped if the tenant is not licensed.

**Remediation action**
- [Get started with Data Loss Prevention](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-learn-about-dlp)
- [Create a DLP policy](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-create-deploy-policy)
- [DLP policy templates](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-policy-reference)
- [DLP and Microsoft 365 Copilot](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/purview/dlp-microsoft365-copilot)
